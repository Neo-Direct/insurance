For Newcomers

How insurance works in Germany for expats

Germany has one of the most comprehensive insurance systems in the world. As an expat or newcomer, understanding the basics helps you stay legally covered — and avoid costly surprises.

🏥

Health insurance is mandatory

Every resident in Germany must have health insurance by law — either public (GKV) or private (PKV). Your visa, income and employer situation determine which option is right for you.

🛡️

Liability covers your daily risks

Personal liability insurance (Haftpflichtversicherung) is not legally required, but considered essential. It covers accidental damage you cause to others — from breaking a neighbour's window to injuring someone.

Avoid These

Common insurance mistakes newcomers make in Germany

Small missteps when setting up insurance in Germany can cost hundreds of euros — or leave you without cover at the worst moment.

⏰

Waiting too long to register for health insurance

Health insurance is legally required from the moment you register your residence (Anmeldung). Gaps in coverage can result in backdated premiums you still have to pay.

🔒

Locking into a private plan without comparing

Private health insurance (PKV) can be very beneficial — but switching back to public insurance (GKV) later is difficult. Always compare both options with an independent advisor before deciding.

🌐

Using your home country insurance in Germany

Travel or expat insurance from your home country typically doesn't satisfy Germany's legal health insurance requirement and won't cover long-term residency needs.

📋

Skipping liability insurance because it seems optional

In Germany, liability claims can run into tens of thousands of euros. Without coverage, you are personally responsible. At under €10/month, this is one of the best-value products available.
